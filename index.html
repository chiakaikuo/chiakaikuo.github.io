About

Exploring amplitudes through geometry

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Physics in Munich. My work focuses on modern on-shell methods in quantum field theory, especially the geometric formulation of scattering amplitudes and correlation functions.

During my Ph.D. at National Taiwan University, supervised by Song He and Yu-Tin Huang, I studied loop amplitudes in ABJM theory and their realization as differential forms associated with novel positive geometries.

Research

Main directions

Positive geometry for scattering amplitudes

I study how amplitudes can be represented as canonical differential forms on positive geometries, with a particular focus on ABJM theory and its amplituhedron-like structures.

Loop amplitudes and integrands

My work develops all-loop and finite-loop constructions for ABJM amplitudes, including unitarity cuts, dimensional reduction, and explicit two-loop integrands.

Correlation functions and correlahedra

I am interested in the geometric organization of correlation-function integrands, chamber decompositions, leading singularities, and pure conformal integral bases.

Cosmological wavefunction coefficients

Recent work extends orthogonal-Grassmannian ideas toward supersymmetric cosmological wavefunction coefficients and their analytic structure.
